  • Spring

    Spring in Japan is filled with cherry blossoms. They start blooming in the South of the country and make their way up North throughout the Spring season. Make sure to take part in hanami, which is a picnic under the cherry blossom trees and try one of the many sakura treats, like sakura mochi. Take a look at the official "Cherry Blossom Forecast" website here.

  • Summer

    Summer offers the opportunity to visit the Japanese seaside, watch hanabi (fireworks displays) or go to one of the many festivals taking place all over the country. Don a traditional Japanese summer robe, called yukata and try one or two of the many snacks found at the festivals, like fried octopus balls called takoyaki. Also not to miss is the Bamboo Forest in Kyoto during the summer months.

  • Autumn

    Autumn in Japan is about colour! Don't miss out on Japanese Maple Trees that turn a beautiful blood red and a visit to one of the thousands of shrines. Probably the most famous shrine to visit during the autumn foliage season is Fushimi Inari Shrine in Kyoto.

  • Winter

    Winter is the perfect season to head to an onsen town (Hot Spring), like Ginzan Onsen in Yamagata and warm up in the hot water. If you are interested in snow sculptures the Sapporo Snow Festival in Hokkaido will be the place to be. Many New Year festivals take place at shrines and it's the pefect opportunity to make a wish for the New Year and enjoy a hot cup of amazake, which is a sweet drink made from rice.
